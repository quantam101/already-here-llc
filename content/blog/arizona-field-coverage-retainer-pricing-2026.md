## What a retainer is — and is not

A retainer is **reserved operational capacity**: guaranteed response windows, scheduling priority, and a defined block of onsite hours for break/fix, smart hands, and assessments. It is not prepaid project labor. Rollouts, rack builds, cabling, and installations are scoped and quoted separately — usually from our [fixed-price service catalog](/services-catalog) — so your monthly number stays predictable.

That separation matters. Providers who stuff project work into retainer hours either inflate the monthly fee to cover the risk or quietly under-deliver on response times when a project eats the calendar. Keeping the two apart is what lets us guarantee the SLA.

## Who the math works for

- **National dispatch companies** paying one-off market rates for every Phoenix ticket: Essential typically pays for itself at 4–6 tickets a month, before you count the coordination time you stop spending.
- **Out-of-state MSPs** with Arizona clients: a Business retainer costs roughly a third of a fully-burdened field hire, with no recruiting, no truck, and no bench risk.
- **Multi-site operators** (restaurant groups, retail, clinics): included assessments and asset inventory mean your sites get healthier every quarter, not just patched.
