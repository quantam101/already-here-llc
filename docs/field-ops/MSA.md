# MASTER SERVICE AGREEMENT

> **DRAFT — for review by an Arizona-licensed attorney before execution.**

This Master Service Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of **[Effective
Date]** by and between **Already Here LLC**, an Arizona limited liability
company ("Provider"), and **[Client Legal Name]**, a **[State/Entity Type]**
("Client").

## 1. Services
Provider will perform field technology services described in one or more
Retainer Agreements or Statements of Work ("Service Documents") executed under
this Agreement. Each Service Document is incorporated by reference. In case of
conflict, the Service Document controls for its engagement.

## 2. Term and Termination
2.1 This Agreement begins on the Effective Date and continues until terminated.
2.2 Either party may terminate for convenience on thirty (30) days' written
notice; active Service Documents run to their own end dates unless separately
terminated.
2.3 Either party may terminate for material breach uncured within fifteen (15)
days of written notice.
2.4 Client remains responsible for fees for services performed through the
effective date of termination.

## 3. Fees and Payment
3.1 Fees are stated in each Service Document; standalone work follows the
Provider Rate Card in effect at time of order.
3.2 Invoices are due **net 15** from invoice date. Retainer fees are invoiced
monthly in advance; overage hours and project work are invoiced monthly in
arrears.
3.3 Late amounts accrue 1.5% per month or the maximum permitted by law.
Provider may suspend services on accounts more than fifteen (15) days past due
after written notice.
3.4 Fees exclude applicable taxes, which are Client's responsibility (excluding
taxes on Provider's income).

## 4. Scheduling, Access, and Client Responsibilities
4.1 Client will provide safe site access, badging/escort where required, a
point of contact, and accurate scope information.
4.2 Visits canceled with less than four (4) business hours' notice, or where
access is not available on arrival, are billable at the applicable minimum
dispatch fee.

## 5. Independent Contractor
Provider is an independent contractor. Nothing creates an employment, agency,
partnership, or joint-venture relationship. Provider controls the manner and
means of performing services.

## 6. Confidentiality
Each party will protect the other's non-public business, technical, and client
information with at least reasonable care, use it only to perform under this
Agreement, and return or destroy it on request. This section survives
termination for three (3) years; trade secrets are protected for as long as
they remain trade secrets.

## 7. Data and Security
Provider will follow Client's posted site security and data-handling policies
made available to Provider in advance, will not access Client systems beyond
the authorized scope, and will promptly report any suspected security incident
arising from Provider's work.

## 8. Warranties
8.1 Provider warrants services will be performed in a professional and
workmanlike manner consistent with industry standards.
8.2 Client's exclusive remedy for breach of 8.1 is re-performance of the
affected services, or if re-performance is not practicable, a refund of fees
paid for the affected services.
8.3 EXCEPT AS STATED IN 8.1, SERVICES ARE PROVIDED "AS IS" AND PROVIDER
DISCLAIMS ALL OTHER WARRANTIES, EXPRESS OR IMPLIED, INCLUDING MERCHANTABILITY
AND FITNESS FOR A PARTICULAR PURPOSE.

## 9. Limitation of Liability
9.1 NEITHER PARTY IS LIABLE FOR INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL,
OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, OR LOST PROFITS, REVENUE, OR DATA.
9.2 EACH PARTY'S TOTAL LIABILITY UNDER THIS AGREEMENT IS CAPPED AT THE FEES
PAID OR PAYABLE BY CLIENT IN THE TWELVE (12) MONTHS PRECEDING THE CLAIM.
9.3 The caps in 9.1–9.2 do not apply to breaches of Section 6
(Confidentiality), a party's indemnification obligations, gross negligence, or
willful misconduct.

## 10. Insurance
Provider maintains commercial general liability insurance of at least
$1,000,000 per occurrence and will provide a certificate of insurance on
request. [Adjust limits/coverages per client requirements before signature.]

## 11. Indemnification
Each party will defend and indemnify the other against third-party claims for
bodily injury, death, or tangible property damage to the extent caused by the
indemnifying party's negligence or willful misconduct in performing under this
Agreement.

## 12. Non-Solicitation
During the term and for twelve (12) months after, neither party will solicit
for employment the other party's personnel who performed under this Agreement,
except through general postings not targeted at such personnel.

## 13. General
13.1 **Governing law:** Arizona, without regard to conflicts rules. Venue:
state or federal courts located in Maricopa County, Arizona.
13.2 **Assignment:** Neither party may assign without written consent, except
to a successor in a merger or sale of substantially all assets.
13.3 **Force majeure:** Neither party is liable for delay caused by events
beyond reasonable control.
13.4 **Notices:** In writing to the addresses on the signature block; email
notice is effective on confirmed receipt. Provider notice address:
dispatch@alreadyherellc.com.
13.5 **Entire agreement:** This Agreement plus Service Documents is the entire
agreement and supersedes prior discussions. Amendments must be written and
signed. Client purchase-order terms have no effect.
